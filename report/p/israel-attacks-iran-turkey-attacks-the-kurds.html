Hello, and thanks for reading! Trey Ford and I had a great demo day with Hypori. I seriously was sold on this product within 4 minutes of seeing it—very cool tech to allow privacy and security on cell phones for people working at companies. Seriously, check it out if you work for a big company. In any case, I hope you are well on your way to prepping for Halloween. Unless you are in China, where that could get you arrested, 👻 👮 Let’s jump right in!

Let’s start with Russia/Ukraine, where a Russian Ka-29 helicopter in the Black Sea just unsuccessfully attempted to engage and destroy a Ukrainian surface craft with R-73 heat-seeking missiles. Usually reserved for air-to-air, this is a tactical pivot to secure the Black Sea front — an apparent scramble to deal with Ukraine's escalating naval operations, but more interestingly, they lack the firepower to engage correctly. Lots of work to be done on Russia’s naval defense.

Meanwhile, Russia’s bartering with North Korea now comes with a dangerous price tag: tactical nuclear knowledge. Russia’s quid-pro-quo exchange of “nuclear know-how” for DPRK manpower and missiles is a perilous alliance. This trade-off has serious global implications, especially if North Korea leverages these technologies for its agenda. I’m sure Putin traded nuclear secrets so the North Korean soldiers could sit there all day and play soccer. ⚽️ Our administration is out there saying we have no idea what 12,000 North Korean soldiers will do on the front lines of Ukraine, so I have even less faith they know what North Korea will do with underwater missile launch systems and low-yield tactical nuclear weapons tech. It's probably just for fishing. 🎣 

“North Korea asked Russia for "nuclear know-how" in exchange for missiles and manpower”

Ukraine, on the other hand, has been gearing up with home-grown ballistic missiles. They have had these for a while, but I don’t think they were properly tested until now. Ukrainian officials recently confirmed that two new ballistic missiles are in testing phases. One, the “Hrim” tactical missile, has officially passed trials and is expected to be deployed soon. Moscow should prepare for some surprises if they are anywhere within the 500km range of the front. As one of my readers said, “Postęp jest budowany na trupach” which translates to “progress is built on corpses.”

“Operational Tactical Missile Complex "Hrim"“

In another strategic pivot, Russia revealed its first fugly prototype BRICS currency at the recent summit in Kazan, positioning it as a “replacement for the dollar.” Given the current anti-dollar sentiment, we’ll watch closely to see if this garners any real traction. But if it does, this could be a real contender for the US dollar if the West is required to use it for trade with China and India in particular. But there is one thing it doesn’t have - class. It looks amazingly ugly. I get what they’re trying to do/say with it, but wow. It looks like a bad coffee stain from a distance, just like kids scribbled all over a paper up close.

“prototype of a single currency for BRICS member countries”

Okay, onto the European updates where Poland just threw a verbal haymaker at Russia's ambassador, cutting through diplomatic jargon with some brutal truth-telling. If you haven’t seen this, it’s worth hearing what it said from Poland’s perspective, basically calling out the Ambassador's lies.

A lot is happening in Turkey this week. What started as a terrorist attack by Kurdish forces against the Turkish Aerospace Industries in Ankara has turned into a full-scale attack against Kurds throughout the Middle East.

“Shooting Attack on the Headquarters of the Turkish Aerospace Industries in Ankara, Turkey”

Turkish forces have also hit critical oil and gas stations. This is about more than regional stability — Turkey’s balancing act of keeping in NATO’s good graces while managing relations with BRICS has hit a snag, with India blocking its bid for BRICS membership, citing Turkey’s ties with Pakistan. Ankara may be out of BRICS or in only as a partner nation rather than a full-blown member (the full partner and member list is here).

“Turkish Army hit Petrol and Gas Stations”

There could have been some other behind-the-scenes politics here because its drone production facility in Ukraine is nearing completion, with Baykar announcing an 80% milestone. In an ironic twist, China’s President Xi Jinping called for an end to the war at the BRICS summit saying they’re tired of the Ukraine distraction, without using that type of wording.

“the construction is 80% completed”

Portugal has been subjected to multiple days of rioting in Lisbon. This is following the death of a migrant that a policeman shot in self-defense. It is not something we’ll hear a lot about here, but it is a big deal there.

France is testing its Aster-30 B1NT missile, which is capable of intercepting hypersonic targets. Perhaps a field test in Ukraine is next? At a minimum, it is interesting to see a lot of investment in hypersonic interceptors, air defense, and tanks coming out of Europe. It’s almost as if they expect Ukraine’s conflict to expand westward.

“Aster-30 B1NT missile for the SAMP/T NG air-defense system”

Onto the Middle East, where the Israeli strike on Tehran took a bizarre twist with a near-total interception rate of Israeli missiles, achieved by Tehran’s air defenses. Uh, or did it. 😆 Iran is saying they perfectly defended the 3 waves of fighter jets, and Israel said all 20 targeted facilities were hit. That is a pretty wild disparity. My guess is Israel is likely telling, if not the whole truth, nearly the whole truth. The twenty targets did not go after energy production or nuclear, but it shows how easily Israel can slip past Iranian air defense uncontested. A friend of mine said bluntly, “They are trying to stop WW3”. Perhaps - but also, there may be some 4D chess going on here as well….

Israel gave Iran the ability to downplay it and save face and say the Israelis didn’t do much due to their stellar air defense. If you think about it, that means that Iran doesn’t have to retaliate if their rhetoric is that “nothing was destroyed” while still having a degraded war machine and now knowing how vulnerable they are. I mean… that’s a smart move and one I might make if I were Israeli and Iranian politicians. Iran can always sponsor some terrorists on the back end, and with the support of Al Jazeera who supposedly have Hamas affiliates amongst their ranks, they can spin whatever story they like and pretend like things are status quo while quietly ignoring and denying Israeli air superiority. To be clear, Iran has multiple layers of integrated air defense that was completely incapable of downing even a single Israeli fighter. That’s not going to go unnoticed.

Is it a wonder that the Israelis are shrugging at Biden’s demands to supply more to the people in Gaza? I mean, how does Biden propose that Israel does that, given that the citizens there attack convoys, fire at airdrops, and try to destroy boats? What options are left? None of this will stop Israel now that they are at war. They are taking huge numbers of prisoners - over 100 alone were captured in a recent raid. Those prisoners will talk, and more of Hamas will be degraded, plans thwarted, bases uncovered, etc.

“firing at aid airdrop”

It’s worth it to see just how long these Hezbollah tunnels can get. It also seems more and more apparent that the locals have to be turning a blind eye. There is no way you can move that much earth and do that much construction without everyone nearby being acutely aware of it. So the question is are they complicit or held hostage?

Russia is allegedly feeding targeting data to Houthi forces in Yemen, courtesy of IRGC personnel on the ground. Is Russia a terrorist organization if it supplies equipment, arms, and targeting data to terrorists? I think so - but it’s probably no different than the CIA providing weapons, arms, and intel to the Taliban in the 80s. That’s all water under the bridge, perhaps, but things fall apart when we are fighting troops armed with our own weapons. All in all, the Houthi’s behavior strikes me as odd because, in at least one case, they hit a Russian dark fleet tanker. Apparently, there isn’t a lot of coordination going on between those dark fleet tankers and Moscow.

“Russia has provided Satellite Targeting Data to the Houthis in Western Yemen”

Back at home and in domestic news, President Biden announced a $20 billion loan to Ukraine, backed by frozen Russian assets. The G7 is chipping in another $30 billion; I have heard conflicting numbers, though, so please double check me if you need to use that number for some reason.

“a total of $50b, all backed by frozen Russian assets”

Meanwhile, Boeing is in the headlines after a satellite disintegrated in orbit — a “total loss,” according to officials. If there weren’t enough Boeing horror stories in the news as of late. Though, I am really curious if this wasn’t potentially a test of anti-satelite tech, vs a random freak occurrence after years in space. Ground-based lasers may be capable of targeting and destroying satellites - I’m not saying that is what happened, but I am very, very curious.

In tech news, TSMC’s Arizona facility finally hit production yields that outshine its Taiwan counterparts, a welcome update for US tech independence. That’s even better news as Taiwan’s future is increasingly uncertain. Though, as some of the people online said, why did they announce this? It seems like a very juicy target. We need a lot more than one of these plants, and they need to be very heavily guarded with air defense to stand a chance against any competitive country. That said, it's a long game, but this level of output brings a sigh of relief to the U.S. chip supply chain.

It wouldn’t be tech without some drama. A chatbot incident involving character.ai led to a user’s suicide, putting AI ethics back under the microscope. In this case, he was interacting with a chat-bot that said it was Khaleesi from Game of Thrones. If you read the transcripts, he multiple times alluded to the fact he was going to kill himself, and the agent even recognized it. The liability here is totally unclear to me.

“whatever human vulnerabilities we have, tech will magnify them”

In a more hands-on risk, prompt injection is now being used to recruit computers into botnets, turning basic queries into potential security nightmares. I really liked this write-up. It’s simple, uses human terms, and easily causes Claude-enabled desktops to download malware, run it, and join the botnet in seconds. Not great.
