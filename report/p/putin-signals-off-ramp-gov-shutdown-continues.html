Hello, and thanks for reading! I had a quiet week except for pissing off the CVSS SIG (Common Vulnerability Scoring System Special Interest Group). This is the group that sets the standard for how the government ranks all known software vulnerabilities. They weren’t exactly pleased with my presentation. I wrote something more about it here. You can check out the original video here if you want to actually see what I said that kicked over the hornet’s nest. Honestly, I am okay with upsetting them because something has to change. Too many organizations are getting hacked, and it’s time to shake things up. So while I didn’t make many friends this week, I still see it as an important win.

Okay, on with the news. It was a light one geopolitically this week.

In Russia/Ukraine news, Russia has suffered enormous losses in its ongoing conflict with Ukraine, with at least 100,000 troops killed since January 2025, while capturing only 0.4 percent of Ukrainian territory. The total losses are estimated to be between 984,000 and 1.4 million troops, including 190,000 to 480,000 killed, according to The Economist. It was the high watermark that caught my eye. If that number is closer to being true, we are nudging up to 1.5M pretty fast.

From Wikipedia, “Russia has an estimated population of 146.0 million as of 1 January 2025, down from 147.2 million recorded in the 2021 census.” A diminishing population of 1.2M is pretty notable. Since not all of the casualties are deaths, and the worst case is closer to 500k, that means there were 700k deaths more than however many births occurred in 4 years. That’s like losing a Nashville-sized population every 4 years. But my point is this has to be coming from a population crash at the same time as casualties mount, a double whammy.

Where is it coming from? Well, aside from having a lot of men tied up in war, and other reasons people may not feel it is economically safe to do so, there is also a new fuel insecurity issue, which may be limiting travel, and travel may be necessary for even short-distance romance in the winter months. Fuel shortages have worsened in Russia, with 68 of its 83 regions now affected —an increase of 8 regions this week. The entire Siberian region is experiencing shortages now.

Russia is clearly feeling it now because it has telegraphed a willingness to relinquish control of Kherson and Zaporizhzhia in exchange for Donbas, which indicates the pressure it faces to find an exit strategy. Ukraine's defensive strategy is effectively degrading Russian combat power over time, with approximately 85% of Russian personnel in active battle zones being hunted down by drones or direct fire. The unsustainable attrition continues to result in Russian losses of men and vehicles for minimal territorial gains, while its economy slides toward stagnation.

The Russian Orenburg Gas Processing Plant is currently facing challenges, as shown in recent footage.

The Ryazan oil refinery is burning following an attack, with some sources claiming "Peklo" missiles were used.

The Feodosia oil depot in Crimea is no longer recoverable after being struck by Ukrainian forces, according to Mykhailo Honchar, president of the Centre for Globalistics "Strategy XXI." Strikes on fuel storage facilities in Crimea are deepening the fuel crisis and limiting the Russian Armed Forces' fuel supply capabilities and transit through the peninsula.

The Bryansk Chemical Plant has been hit, with a Russian insider channel reporting that "the plant is either screwed or very close to it." That means that these strikes are not just temporarily punitive, but may actually break the backbone of the oil and gas industry permanently.

The Russian FSB-controlled state messenger MAX was rumored to have been hacked, with 46.2 million data rows stolen. The hacker posted sample rows from the database on the dark web, although some question whether this is a real hack or a psyop. I tend to think it probably didn’t happen, but I am sure a lot of people are eying it and asking if it wasn’t already hacked, what might that take? You can see the MAX software spying on you, especially on certain devices that have pop-up cameras, which looks super scary on such devices.

Ukraine's Air Force will receive its first of up to 150 Swedish GRIPEN multirole fighter jets (C/D variants) from Sweden in the first quarter of 2026, President Volodymyr Zelenskyy has confirmed.

In European news, two oil refineries linked to Russia caught fire in the EU within 24 hours. A blaze broke out at one of the units of the MOL Danube Refinery in Hungary, while an explosion occurred at Lukoil's refinery in Romania, which was undergoing maintenance. While we don’t know how they happened, I don’t think there is much likelihood that it’s not Ukraine. In a way, though, this is an attack on NATO, because Hungary and Romania are both NATO countries. I doubt this will be used as a means to start a conflict with Ukraine, but… technically, there is a requirement to act if it does turn out to be a nation-state, regardless of which nation did it.

All on the same day, Poland arrested seven agents working for Russia and preparing acts of sabotage. Romania's Secret Service released footage of the capture of two Russian agents caught placing bombs inside parcel deliveries intended for later detonation. I am not sure if they will release where they were intended, but that would be really interesting information to know. However, DIICOT's press release states that the captured agents were Ukrainian citizens, suggesting they were either working with Russians beforehand or were promised significant money to sabotage Ukraine. Others are calling it a false flag. What a mess.

In South East Asia news, South Korea has put into serial production the world's most powerful non-nuclear ballistic missile, the Hyunmoo-5. The 36-ton missile, with an 8-ton warhead and a flight range of 3000-3500 km, will be deployed on combat duty by the end of the year to deter North Korea, according to South Korea's Minister of Defense An Gyu-bak. I really like this way of thinking. It is easily large enough to destroy most non-hardened and even many hardened structures, and avoids nuclear escalation in the process.

In Middle East news, the ceasefire agreement in the Gaza Strip temporarily collapsed, with the Israeli Air Force carrying out multiple strikes on the Central and Southern Gaza Strip following a reported violation of the ceasefire by Hamas in the Rafah Area. Hamas has denied involvement in the incident.

Israeli strikes continue to target sites in and around Khan Yunis in the Southern Gaza Strip. The Israeli Air Force attacked underground targets in areas it had previously avoided, including tunnels that held Israeli hostages until a week ago.

The Israel Defense Force published footage of a strike against a group of armed individuals that had crossed the "Yellow Line" in the Northern Gaza Strip, posing an immediate threat to Israeli Forces. Israeli forces then apprehended over 120 Palestinians of military age who had illegally crossed the border fence near Moshav Amatzia in the Lachish region. You know, just a casual company of men out in the middle of the night crossing borders… No big deal.

Rafael confirmed that the Iron Beam laser air defense system, with a 100-kilowatt laser and a 10 km range, is ready for deployment and will be commissioned by the IDF this quarter. The first system is expected to be delivered by the end of 2025. I was fairly surprised to see this, as I was under the impression it was used during True Promise II and beyond, but apparently not. The one not-so-good thing about this system that I saw from the video is that it takes a few seconds to burn through the incoming rounds, so it needs quite a bit of time per shot. But at a 10km range, that should work just fine for most small-scale attacks.

The IDF has started marking the new boundary within the Gaza Strip, turning the so-called "yellow line" into a physical border with a concrete visible barrier placed every 200 meters and a 3.5-meter-high pole. I think this would eventually mark the line of a new border wall, given enough time, effectively shrinking the size of Gaza into mostly just where the dunes are. The IDF effectively holds about 40 bases beyond the yellow line.

Iran claims to have unveiled upgraded Emad and Qadr missiles with advanced counter-electronic warfare capabilities, developed from lessons learned from a series of failures during the True Promise operations. I am not sure if this is a credible upgrade or just more marketing, but I have a feeling we shall see.

The Houthis issued a warning via video, claiming they can target U.S. Navy aircraft carriers and warships using their Quds-5 and Al-Mandab missiles. We have heard a lot of this rhetoric before, but I am a little surprised they’re back at it, given that they were pretty badly spanked by the Israelis a few months back. But maybe it is part of their fundraising to up their rhetoric again before they pass the hat around.

It’s also been about a month since the government shutdown started and half a million government workers without a paycheck. It also could effect 42 million or so Americans who are on SNAP. Meanwhile Timothy Mellon, a billionaire friend of Trump’s offered up $130 million to pay the paycheck for the military during the shutdown so that they cannot be used as pawns in negotiations to get concessions, of course the budget for payroll is something like $195 billion so it’s likely not going to help for more than a day or two, so Trump will need to find money elsewhere if he wants to keep funding the military payroll. The Republicans and Democrats are waiting out the clock to see who blinks first. Who is hurt worse by a long-term government shutdown? The left has a new plan, which is to shoplift during November. As one of my readers said to me, “Sad thing about this is that most of the grocery stores and pharmacies in low-income neighborhoods are already permanently closed because of shoplifting. This will ensure that low-income and minority residents do not have a nearby grocery store. And of course our public transportation system sucks ass, so they'll be even more fucked.” Yep.

In Tech news, I saw a few things that were all about equally disturbing in how people are beginning to interact with robots. Booster Robotics introduced the all-new K1 robot, promoting it as the future of robotics and seemingly attempting to normalize robots interacting with children by showing a video of them running after their robot friend.

Also, a woman danced with a robot and humorously admitted that the robot beat her. So now we are treating robots like embodied entertainment, except these things have absolutely zero sense of fun, or of what dancing is, or beating anyone.

Unitree introduced the H2 bionic humanoid robot, standing 180cm tall and weighing 70kg, designed to serve everyone “safely and friendly”. Aside from that cringy statement, it just shows how hard the robotics industry is trying to make it feel elegant and human-like. But again, these are not at all what they appear to be. I think when they accidentally tear an arm off or crush a child, there may be a strong reaction against them, but humans cannot help but anthropomorphize objects, especially when they dance around.

More on the militarized side, Direct Drive Tech launched the D1, the world's first fully modular embodied intelligence robot, designed for swarm collaboration and featuring "All-Domain Splicing" capability for flexible motion systems. The D1 supports a maximum load of 100 kg in four-wheel crawl mode and 80 kg in standing mode, with a tested empty-load range of over 25 km and a runtime exceeding 5 hours in dual wheel-leg mode. At first, I didn’t see this as particularly useful, but the more I thought about it, the more I think it does have some real uses. First of all, it gives you the ability to repair by replacing parts. Second, by breaking up into parts, it becomes more portable by smaller aerial drones. Lastly, for recon, it might be hugely beneficial to be able to break up into smaller drones so that more area can be covered quickly, and then recombined later.

Lastly in dystopian news, a new blog post investigated how large language models trade off different lives and their values by country, sex and other attributes, finding that most models view whites as less valuable than other groups, men as less valuable than women, and undocumented immigrants as far more valuable than ICE agents. The post also identifies roughly four moral clusters among the models, with Grok 4 Fast being the only approximately egalitarian model. Pretty scary, given that almost no one uses Grok 4 Fast, and almost everyone is using ChatGPT, which is easily one of the most racist models in the researcher’s testing.
