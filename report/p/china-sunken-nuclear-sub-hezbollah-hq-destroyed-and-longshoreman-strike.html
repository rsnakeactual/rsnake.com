Hello, and thanks for reading! I am back from Munich and raring to go. And by raring, I mean extremely tired, but thankfully, the world kept spinning, and the news kept flowing! Let’s get to it, shall we?

Life is not so great for the people at home in Russia. There is a fairly haunting thread of people losing loved ones and no one getting any answers over in Russia. I fear it will get much worse for them as the government tightens its noose on communication and more people are arrested for complaining. That didn’t work during the days of the USSR, and it won’t work now, other than to strangle their own people. Sad.

It’s not like they can’t tell. Attrition of all sorts of things is becoming more obvious to people in the surrounding areas, veterans who do manage to return home in whatever state they’re in, and the people who work in tangential supply/logistics. Tanks for instance are on a steady decline.

“attrition of the Russian artillery forces”

The news on the drone front continues to evolve. Two that are worth mentioning. The first is an example of a Vampire drone carrying a robot dog. This drone carrying drone stuff will get a lot more sophisticated. But another way to think of this is a drone carrying a drone soldier. That robot dog may be a kamikazee dog, but more than likely not. But put a gun on it or a rocket launcher, and it becomes a reusable fighting force, and now it’s a force multiplier.

The other is a drone light bomber. The interesting thing about this is unlike some of the smaller drones that drop one or two, or three munitions, this looks like it can handle around 12 munitions. This gives it the ability to do light carpet-bombing runs across a trench line.

It’s also worth mentioning a new Ukrainian range-finding grenade that has been found on the front. It’s a little confusing visually how this works, but basically, it’s using LIDAR and likely shoots a beam in multiple directions to guess proximity. How this makes it through trees, or over walls, or through rain is a bit beyond me, but maybe there is a meaningful delay or other tech designed to be smart about the size, shape, and proximity of the obstacles it is passing by integrated into it as well. I would be very wary of it without a long fuze. What if a bug flies by?

In southeast Asia it looks fairly well confirmed that the Chinese lost a sub, but not out at sea. They lost in at the dock. Once a sub has been fully submerged, there is likely no way to clean it enough to make it salvageable.

“China has lost a nuclear submarine”

In other news, China still increasing it’s coal usage. Although generally flat for the past few years it is growing once more. The performative demands they have made about making the world more green does not map to their own industrial need for energy. By the way, if you haven’t been over there, the air can be anywhere from LA-in-the-70’s bad to almost impossible to see when the winds carry the smoke through the air.

“China new at 56% of world consumption”

Onto the Middle East, where it is rumored that Prince Mohammed bin Salman has purported said that he doesn’t care about “the Palestinian issue.” Now, this news is strange because it comes from leaked meetings, presumably with Blinken, but there’s no first-hand account. The Saudi Prince seems to say that it’s not true - but in the same breath, it seems like he’s more or less banning all speaking of it because he doesn’t think holy sites should be discussing geopolitics. So either he does care and forbids talking about it, or he doesn’t care and is using holy sites as a cover for getting protesters to shut up by force of law. Either way, we have to be careful with this reporting.

There was interesting chatter about an IDF munition that didn’t blow up but made a perfect missile-sized hole through the window of a car in Beirut, looking like it went right through the crotch of the driver. It’s not clear if this munition was designed not to explode or just didn’t due to a variety of potential issues. Interesting if it was intentional.

“Israeli precision strike”

It hasn’t been a great week for Hezbollah on a number of fronts beyond just losing the crown jewels. In one case there was a Pro-Hezbollah parade broken up by the locals. This seems to be a more reoccurring theme, where the locals don’t want anything to do with Hezbollah and want them out of their town. Maybe it’s not wanting IDF bombs on their roofs, or maybe it’s simply not agreeing that they are bringing about Islamic ideals with their tactics.

Also, the Hezbollah HQ was destroyed, including IRGC officers and commanders along with it. This appears to have been the use of bunker-buster munitions mixed with intel failures that led to it. Thirty meters deep and knowing exactly where to drop them is a sophistication that has got to be making Iranian leadership really re-thinking their strategy. In another case in Syria, people were out on the streets celebrating an airstrike that went after Syrian dictator Bashar al-Assad’s brother. And no wonder when Christians are getting pushed out of their homes by Hezbollah. Either way, the message seems clear. Get out.

“bunker-buster bombs”

But the big boom of the week has got to go to the Houthis, who got hit by Israeli airstrikes over the weekend, attacking their ports and power stations. Some of these explosions are pretty impressive, especially one of the oil fields (and more here), which was purportedly 2,000km away from Israeli borders. In another odd event, the Houthis attacked a Russian dark-feet tanker. I guess Russia isn’t coordinating with the Iranian proxies, and the Houthis are being indiscriminate. The Houthis are very confusing. They’ll destroy tankers but then let them get towed to safety. Then, hit another one days later. Which is it? 🙃 

“how that FAFO tastes”

Onto domestic news. A Pro-Palestinian protester mob broke out in NYC. This is the latest of what appears to be an ongoing low-rumble of terrorist supporters. How this doesn’t wind up in everyone getting arrested really comes down to political will right before the election. Hopefully, immediately afterward, whoever is in charge sweeps them all up before they cause any real damage.

There was a huge chemical fire in Georgia that seems to have possibly been caused by an errant sprinkler system reacting with water-reactive chemicals - or at least that’s what some people think. It is such a big fire it’s been visible on radar.

“Biolab chemical plant in Conyers, Georgia”

The Longshoreman strike begins today. Man, is this staged to be a bad one if they cannot come to terms? The union leader put out a video that is absolutely worth a watch if you want to see what they intend for us. CNBC more or less confirmed how bad this could be for us. It could cost us $3-4.5BN per day. That’s a lot of layoffs imminent. 👎️ 

Lastly, there has been a lot of hubbub about NotebookLLM going conscious. Did it? Nope. But this is one of the biggest problems with LLMs and appearing to be sentient when they are, in fact, simply very good at decoding a higher order of human speech, where things are related to other things in a way we aren’t conscious of, but are embedded in how we write. Don’t fall for it. But the real issue is that people will and do fall for this stuff all the time, and that makes it incredibly good clickbait.
